# Exam Prep — Workshop A

**Exam:** Saturday, June 13, 09:00–12:00, hall L10 · 5 ECTS (project + written exam).

## Stated learning objectives (from the program)
By the end of the workshop you should be able to:

1. **Dimensions of robotic complexity** — be familiar with several dimensions (sensing, actuation, computation, communication), list **metrics** to quantify/compare them, and relate different approaches in those terms.
   → `sensor-lattices`, `information-spaces`, `embodiment-x-minimalism`, `information-invariants`
2. **Ideas & their history** — connect current robotics ideas to their historical antecedents (Braitenberg, sensorless manipulation, behaviour-based robotics).
   → `braitenberg-vehicles`, `minimalism-as-life-philosophy`, `tray-tilting`
3. **Information spaces** — understand the concept and list several examples.
   → `information-spaces`, `readings/reading-04`
4. **Filtering vs. planning** — relate and contrast filtering and planning problems.
   → `filters-plans-and-reduction-algorithms`, `refinements-and-dynamic-programming`
5. **Intractable complexity-reduction problems** — show familiarity with reduction problems known to be computationally hard.
   → `filters-plans-and-reduction-algorithms` (combinatorial filter minimization)
6. **Analyze novel problems** — propose simple robots that use resources in innovative ways.
   → the whole workshop; practice via the team project

## Self-check questions (grow this list through the week)
- Define an **information space**. Give 3 example sensors and their **preimages**.
- Walk through the **predict/correct** update for a **nondeterministic I-state**.
- Contrast a **nondeterministic** I-state (set) with a **probabilistic** one (distribution).
- What is a **sufficient I-map** and why does it matter?
- Why is **combinatorial filter minimization** hard?
- Give an example of replacing **sensing with mechanics** (sensorless manipulation).
- Explain a **Braitenberg vehicle** and the "law of uphill analysis / downhill synthesis".
- What does **co-design** mean and why is it different from designing body then controller?
