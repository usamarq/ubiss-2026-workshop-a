## TL;DR
A short, framing lecture: minimalism as a **research stance and aesthetic** — strip away the inessential to reveal what a task *fundamentally* requires.

## Core ideas
- **"Less is more"** as a design discipline, not just a constraint.
- Echoes broader ideas: **Occam's razor** (prefer the simplest explanation), and robotics antecedents like Brooks' *"intelligence without representation"* (behaviour-based robotics).
- Removing a resource (a sensor, memory, computation) and asking *"does the task still get solved?"* is both a **scientific probe** and a **design method**.
- Simplicity tends to buy **robustness, cost, legibility, and analyzability**.

## Why it matters
This is the "why" behind the technical machinery — the mindset the rest of the week formalizes.
