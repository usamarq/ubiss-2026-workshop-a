## TL;DR
The framing lecture: what "minimalism in robotics" means, why it matters, and how the week is organized.

## Core ideas
- **The central question:** what is the _minimum_ a robot needs — across **sensing, actuation, computation, communication** — to accomplish a given task?
- **Two motivations** (from the workshop synopsis):
  1. **Scientific** — simple robots reveal the *fundamental resources* a task requires.
  2. **Engineering** — simplicity helps **manufacturability & marketability** (cheaper, more robust robots).
- The workshop turns "resources" into **dimensions/metrics** so robots can be **compared** ("is robot A more powerful than robot B?").
- Tools used during the week are both **conceptual/theoretical** (compare robots, prove limits) and **computational** ("compress"/simplify a robot).
- Expect **impossibility results & complexity** (some simplifications are provably hard).
