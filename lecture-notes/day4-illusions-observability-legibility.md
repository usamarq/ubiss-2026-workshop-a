# Illusions, Observability & Legibility

## TL;DR
Three lenses on **what a robot's behaviour reveals or conceals** — to itself (observability), to observers (legibility), and the gap between appearance and reality (illusions).

## Key concepts
- **Observability** — can the hidden **state** be inferred from the **outputs/observations**? (control-theory notion). In I-space terms: does the I-state collapse enough to pin down what matters?
- **Legibility** — designing motion/behaviour so an **observer can read the robot's intent**. The *constructive* opposite of Braitenberg's "uphill analysis" problem; central to human-robot interaction.
- **Illusions** — a robot can **appear to know or do more (or less)** than it really does. Behaviour can be engineered to create an impression, or to **hide** capability. Braitenberg's over-attribution of emotion is exactly such an illusion.

## Why it matters for *minimalism*
A minimal robot may *look* sophisticated (illusion) or be made to *broadcast* intent cheaply (legibility) — appearance and information are themselves design resources.

## Connections
- Pays off `braitenberg-vehicles` (uphill/downhill law).
- Observability links to `information-spaces` (when does sensing determine the state?).
