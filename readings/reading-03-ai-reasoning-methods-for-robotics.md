# Reading 3 — AI Reasoning Methods for Robotics

> Beetz M, Chatila R, Hertzberg J & Pecora F (2016). *AI Reasoning Methods for Robotics.* In: Siciliano & Khatib (eds.), **Springer Handbook of Robotics**, 2nd ed., pp. 329–356.
> Status: 🟡 basics pre-filled

## TL;DR
**Symbolic + probabilistic reasoning** for robots — how to represent knowledge and decide *what to do* at the task level (the "high-level brain").

## Key topics
- **Knowledge representation (KR):** logic, **ontologies**, semantic maps; representing objects, places, actions.
- **Task planning:** action models (preconditions/effects, e.g. **STRIPS / PDDL**); search for action sequences to reach a goal.
- **Reasoning under uncertainty:** combining logic with probability.
- **Temporal & spatial reasoning;** constraint-based reasoning.
- **Plan execution & monitoring;** integrating symbolic reasoning with perception and control (the symbol-grounding gap).

## Relevance to the workshop
- The **deliberative / high-level** complement to the workshop's **minimal, sub-symbolic, reactive** flavour (cf. Braitenberg, behaviour-based robotics).
- Useful contrast: heavy representation & reasoning vs. "intelligence without representation."

## 🎯 Likely exam points
- Basics of KR&R for robots.
- What a task planner needs: states, actions (preconditions/effects), goals.
